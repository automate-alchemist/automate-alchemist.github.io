Some of the major software development lifecycle models are:

<img src='/images/new.jpeg' >


* <b>Iterative Model :</b> The Iterative Model focuses on developing and deploying one feature at a time, repeating the same series of activities in multiple cycles. This repetition allows refinement through each development cycle, enabling continuous improvement and flexibility to incorporate changes.

*<b> Incremental Model :</b> The Incremental Model involves developing and deploying features incrementally. It requires a well-defined modular architecture upfront, as each new feature builds on the previous one. This approach ensures steady progress while gradually adding functionality.

* <b>Agile Model :</b> The Agile Model emphasizes collaboration, customer feedback, and adaptability. Development and coding take place simultaneously in iterative cycles, enabling quick delivery of value. Agile fosters flexibility and responsiveness to changing requirements, making it ideal for dynamic environments.

*<b> Spiral Model :</b> The Spiral Model combines iterative development with risk analysis at every phase. Each cycle involves planning, risk resolution, development, and evaluation, allowing teams to refine the system progressively. While it is suitable for complex and high-risk projects, it can be expensive and challenging to manage.

*<b> Waterfall Model :</b> The Waterfall Model is a linear and sequential approach where all requirements are collected before development begins. Each phase must be completed before the next starts, making it simple and easy to understand for smaller projects with well-defined requirements.

* <b>V-Model :</b> The V-Model focuses on validation and verification by aligning testing activities with each stage of development. It ensures that the software meets business requirements and maintains quality by verifying and validating at every step.


<b>Some other type of Development Models are: </b>

* <b>Prototype Model : </b> The Prototype Model is centered around creating a working prototype to gather feedback and refine requirements before developing the final system. This model is highly effective in projects where user needs are unclear or evolving, as it allows stakeholders to visualize functionalities and provide input early in the development process.

* <b>Rapid Application Development (RAD) Model : </b>The Rapid Application Development (RAD) Model prioritizes speed and flexibility by focusing on rapid prototyping and iterative development. It involves active user participation and relies on reusable components, which enables faster delivery of high-quality systems. This model is ideal for projects requiring quick turnaround times and frequent updates based on user feedback.
